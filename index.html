What Has Been Witnessed

Campaign Journal

A record of the darkness endured, and the truths uncovered.

I
The Beginning

The Mist & the Wolves

The Wayfarer Tavern · Svalich Road · The Iron Gates

The group met in The Wayfarer, a tavern where an uneasy military presence made clear that not everyone was welcome. A conflict with the king's soldiers ended the night early — and a Vistani woman with a deck of tarokka cards ended it with something else entirely.

She read their futures without being asked. The cards spoke of five things needed to face a lord of darkness: a book of secrets, a sacred symbol, a weapon of light, an unexpected ally, and the place where the enemy waits. She told them they would be drawn to that place whether they wished it or not.

The Mists Take Them

They didn't cross a border. The mist absorbed them. One moment The Wayfarer, the next a dirt road through a silent forest where the birds had forgotten how to sing. Every attempt to leave the path returned them to it. Barovia does not release what it takes.

On the road they found a corpse — a man half-devoured by animals, still clutching a letter. Sarda read it aloud. It was from Kolyan Indirovich, Burgomaster of Barovia Village, a desperate plea for help against a vampire no one could stop. The letter was genuine. The man was already dead.

The Iron Gates

The forest parted enough to reveal enormous iron gates flanked by crumbling statues. The gates opened on their own as the party approached. When they looked back, the gates had closed. The valley had sealed itself around them.

The Wolves

Two encounters with wolves on the road. The first pack was ordinary — dangerous but natural. The second was led by a dire wolf, a creature built like a siege engine and just as hard to bring down. When it finally fell, those who stood closest saw something wrong: a faint red glow in its eyes, fading as it exhaled its last breath. As if something had been looking through those eyes and decided to look elsewhere.

Laucian examined the body. He found no magic. Whatever it was, it had already left.

What We Know
  • The mist is a one-way door — there is no leaving
  • The Tarokka reading was genuine — five objects, one enemy
  • Burgomaster Kolyan Indirovich asked for help. He is already dead.
  • Something was controlling — or watching through — the dire wolf
  • Strahd already knows they are here
Still Unanswered
  • What exactly was behind the dire wolf's eyes?
  • Where are the five objects the cards spoke of?
  • Who — or what — is Ireena Kolyana?
II
New Faces, Old Ruins

Kaelos, Lancelot & the Durst House

The Ruined Farmsteads · The Road to the Village · Death House

The ruins between the iron gates and the village should have been empty. They weren't. The sound of a flute — then screaming — then a satyr flying through a doorway headfirst into the mud.

Kaelos was already half-dead when the group arrived. Skeletons and a rat swarm had cornered him inside what remained of a farmhouse. Sarda ran to him before she knew his name and spent her Lay on Hands to pull him back from the edge. He regained consciousness and immediately cast Thunderwave. No thank you. Just action.

In another ruined building, they heard an animal whimpering. Lancelot — a farm dog, collar still on — was trapped under a collapsed beam. Thokk and Kaelos lifted the beam. Laucian healed him. The dog read the room and decided Sarda was the safest person present. He has not left her side since.

The Dark Carriage

They sheltered in the ruins for the night. During Dara's watch, a black carriage drawn by a single horse came down the road from the direction they were heading. It stopped. It faced the ruins for a long moment. Then it turned and disappeared into the mist. Dara did not wake anyone — noise felt like the wrong move. She told the group in the morning. Whoever drove that carriage knows exactly where they are.

The Map and the House

Kaelos found a leather pouch in the rubble — a strange potion and a map of Barovia without place names. He tried to keep it to himself. The group caught him. Using the map and her ranger instincts, Dara confirmed they were somewhere in the eastern farmlands, far from mountains or lakes. The only way forward was the road.

At midday, Rose and Thorn Durst appeared in the middle of the path — two children, clean clothes, dry eyes, unnaturally still. They said there was a monster in the basement of their house. They said their parents had left. They said they were very scared. They would not go inside.

Inside the Durst House

The mansion was pristine in a valley of rot. Inside: carved wood panels, a trophy room with three stuffed wolves and five mounted deer heads — Dara's expression made her feelings clear. A formal dining room with silver cutlery on the table. Enna and Kaelos began quietly pocketing silverware. The rest of the group considered it. The silverware turned to mud when they tried to take it. First lesson: Barovia does not reward casual greed.

They found a dumbwaiter hidden behind a panel, ascending through the house. They took the stairs. On the second floor landing, a family portrait confirmed what they already half-suspected about the children. In the music room: a piano, a harp, and shelves of black stone sculptures. From a distance, figures of dancers. Up close — skulls.

III
Voices Under the House

The Hidden Study & the Descent

Death House — Upper Floors · The Attic · The Catacombs

In a house that wanted to be feared, Kaelos took out his violin. The melody was soft and out of place — a small act of defiance, or maybe just habit. Laucian told him quietly that noise in this house might wake something below. Thokk admitted the music reminded him of home. For a moment, the house felt almost survivable.

The Library & Durst's Secret

A library on the upper floors held one book that didn't belong — a red volume, too deliberate in its placement. Enna pulled it. A section of shelving swung open into a hidden study.

Inside: a locked chest. Kaelos picked it. A poison needle caught him — minor damage, a warning. The chest held spell scrolls, valuables, documents, and one letter signed by Strahd himself. In it, he mocked the Durst family with casual contempt. The cult they built, the lives they spent — all of it amounted to nothing more than a convenience for him. The house was an offering box. The Dursts were the offering.

The Animated Armour

Third floor. Cold air, thick dust, paintings that seemed to shift when you looked away from them. At the end of a hall: a full suit of plate armour, standing. Enna approached. The armour turned its head and attacked. The fight in the narrow corridor was brutal — Enna was knocked unconscious and hit the floor hard. The group held together. Laucian stabilised her. They finished it.

The Nursemaid & the Ghost

The nursemaid's bedroom door was stuck. Thokk opened it by force. Inside: frozen time, covered furniture, and the spectral form of the nursemaid herself, furious and swinging. Kaelos used one of the scrolls from the hidden study — a spectral weapon materialised and cut through the ghost alongside the rest of the group's attacks. It dissipated.

In the adjacent room, the baby's room: an empty crib, and the nursemaid's decomposed corpse on the floor. Laucian tried to carry it downstairs for proper burial. The front door would not open. The house would not let them leave. Rose and Thorn were no longer visible outside.

The Attic & the Truth

The children's room in the attic held two small beds, forgotten toys, and a dollhouse — a perfect replica of the Durst mansion in miniature. Sarda examined it carefully and it revealed everything: all the hidden passages, all the secret doors, and most importantly, a secret staircase descending directly from this room into the basement.

When Enna opened the toy chest, Rose and Thorn appeared — not as the children on the road, but as ghosts. They did not recognise the group. They told what happened: their parents locked them in this room to protect them from the monster below. Men and women in black robes visited regularly. There was a baby called Walter they never saw. Every time they tried to leave, something pulled them back.

The group promised to go below and face the monster. Thorn panicked. He crossed the room and entered Sarda's body — his childhood terror now running through her, underneath her own will but present, making her reluctant to move far from this room. The group reached level 2. Then the staircase opened before them.

The Catacombs

Below the house, a network of earthen tunnels braced with wet timber. From the moment their feet touched the ground, a chanting began — many voices, no bodies, repeating something none of them could quite make out. The Durst family crypts lined the walls. Gustav's sarcophagus was open and empty. Elisabeth's was closed. Dara slid the lid aside and a swarm of giant centipedes poured out. The group fought in inches, retreating into a ritual dining hall with a stone table and skeletal figures in black robes chained to the walls. Sarda's Divine Strike with Lucero pulverised most of the swarm at once.

From a side door, a ghoul attacked. Then two more. Kaelos improvised — a Minor Illusion of a spiked stone wall sent one of them circling in confusion while the group handled the others. One ghoul took an arrow from Enna directly through the eye and collapsed onto Sarda, pinning her briefly under its weight.

The chanting continued. Deeper.

IV
One Must Die

The Altar & the Escape

Death House — The Deep Basement · The Altar Room · The Flight

Before the altar room, before the decision that would define the session, there was a statue. Strahd carved in dark wood — one hand on a wolf, the other extended. The cult used it for offerings. Thokk tried to set it on fire. Kaelos cast Tasha's Hideous Laughter on him. Thokk spent the next few rounds laughing uncontrollably on the floor.

Gustav Durst

Near what had once been an office — papers, names of the missing, records of what the cult had done here — the ghasts attacked. Two of them, fast and foul-smelling, emerging from the tunnel walls. The fight was close quarters and brutal. On the final perception check, someone recognised the face of one of the ghasts. Gustav Durst. The patriarch in the family portrait, the empty sarcophagus, now this. The cult consumed its own leader in the end.

The Iron Gate

An iron gate with the mechanism on the wrong side blocked the passage deeper. Laucian reached through the bars with Mage Hand and pulled the lever. The gate opened. Before crossing, Dara spent her last spell slot on Detect Evil and Good, reading the altar through the bars. It was there. It was waiting. It was not something that could be reasoned with.

The Altar Room

Eight-by-eight. The perimeter flooded, ankle-deep. A four-by-four platform of dry stone in the centre, the altar on it. And from the altar, the chanting finally made sense: One must die. Over and over. Not a request.

The figures chanting were not fully present in this world — attacks passed through them. The altar wanted a sacrifice to be appeased. The group looked at each other. Someone looked at Lancelot.

Sarda said no. The group said no. Whatever Thorn's fear was doing inside Sarda, on this point there was no hesitation. They turned and walked back through the gate. Laucian used Mage Hand to close it behind them, hoping to slow what came next.

The Shambling Mound

The altar did not wait. The house invoked a Shambling Mound — a mass of animate plant matter and rot, large enough to fill the tunnel. The iron gate barely slowed it. One pull and it was open again, the creature moving with terrible purpose behind them.

The group ran. Basement to ground floor to upper floors and back down — the house rearranged its hostility around them as they moved. Most of the party made it to the front door, which finally opened. Thokk reached the second floor window, tied a rope, and descended last. Below, the Shambling Mound appeared on the balcony. It looked down at them in the mud and rain. Then it went back inside.

It could leave. It chose not to.

Outside

Lancelot was alive. Rose and Thorn's ghosts were not visible. The Death House stood behind them, unchanged and patient. The group took their first full breath of outside air since entering and reached level 3.

What We Know
  • Gustav Durst became a ghast — the cult destroyed its own founder
  • The altar demanded a life. The group refused.
  • The Shambling Mound could have followed them outside. It didn't.
  • The Death House is still standing. Still waiting.
  • Rose and Thorn have not been given the rest they were promised
Still Unanswered
  • What would have happened if they had sacrificed someone?
  • Will Rose and Thorn ever find rest?
  • Is Thorn still inside Sarda?

V
The Valley Opens

The Werewolf & the Village

Svalich Road · The Village of Barovia · The Tavern

The road between the Death House and the village did not offer a quiet walk. A pack of wolves emerged from the treeline, and with them something larger — a werewolf. Not feral, not random. It moved with the deliberate coordination of something receiving orders, and when the group finally brought it down, that impression did not go away. Strahd was still testing them. This was not the last test.

They arrived at the Village of Barovia at dusk. The mist sat low between the houses. Windows were shuttered. Doors were bolted. The few silhouettes they glimpsed disappeared the moment they sensed movement on the road. This is a village that learned long ago that strangers after dark are not a good sign.

The Tavern & Ismark

The only light came from the tavern. Inside they found Ismark Kolyanovich — son of the dead burgomaster, broad-shouldered, carrying the weight of a village on his face. He recognised immediately that they were not from Barovia, and he did not waste time. His father was dead. His sister Ireena was in danger. The attacks on the mansion had been escalating. He needed help he could not find here.

Kaelos, true to form, found his own point of interest in the tavern — a Vistani woman who made clear she was happy to see him and that she expected to see him again, at the Vistani camp by the river. Kaelos filed this away with a smile.

The Burgomaster's Mansion

Ismark walked them to the mansion. In the dark, the damage was visible enough: deep claw marks in the doors, gouges in the shutters, tracks in the mud of the garden that didn't belong to anything natural. Whatever had been coming here was not subtle. It was not trying to be. The attacks were a message as much as an assault, and the message was directed at whoever was inside.

The session ended here, at the threshold of the mansion, with the evidence of Strahd's attention written into every scarred surface.

What We Know
  • The werewolf on the road was under Strahd's control
  • Burgomaster Kolyan Indirovich is dead
  • Ireena Kolyana is being actively targeted by Strahd's forces
  • Ismark wants the group to escort Ireena to Vallaki
  • There is a Vistani camp by the river near the village
People Met

VI
Debts to the Dead

The Burgomaster's Funeral & the Church

The Village of Barovia · The Cemetery · The Church of the Morning Lord

Ireena Kolyana was not what the letter had implied. The burgomaster's plea spoke of someone to be protected. The woman who met them at the mansion door was already protecting herself — composed, direct, and completely aware of the situation she was in. She accepted the group's help because she was not foolish enough to refuse it, not because she needed rescuing.

Ismark's request was straightforward: bury their father before the party left, then escort Ireena to Vallaki. The group discussed it. Not everyone was immediately certain. But the answer was yes.

The Church — Before the Funeral

They entered the church of the Morning Lord before the burial service. Something was audible from below — a sound from the basement, something between an animal and a human voice, intermittent and wrong. They asked Father Donavich about it. He looked at them the way a man looks at something he has decided not to see. He said nothing and returned to his preparations.

The Burial

The cemetery was quiet in the way Barovia is always quiet — not peaceful, just empty of resistance. Father Donavich performed the rites with mechanical precision. He was present in body. Whatever animated him as a priest, as a man, was somewhere else entirely. The village sent almost no one.

Donavich Speaks

After the earth covered Kolyan Indirovich, the father finally talked. His son Doru had followed the monster hunter Rudolph van Richten to Castle Ravenloft, believing they could end Strahd. Doru came back changed — a vampire spawn, barely recognisable, chained in the church basement where his father had been keeping him for months, praying for a miracle that had not come.

Donavich gave the group something useful alongside the grief: a clear account of vampire weaknesses, the tools that could hurt them, the conditions that could kill them. And one more thing — if Strahd were destroyed, Doru would be freed from his control. The spawn do not outlast their master.

The group looked at each other. Killing Strahd was already the implicit objective. Now it had a secondary reward attached to it.

They left Doru chained in the basement. There was nothing else to do.

Sarda at the Church

Sarda felt it most — a father who could not release his son, a son who could not return. Faith as a structure assumes the structure holds. In Barovia, the Morning Lord's church had been standing for a long time, but the Morning Lord had been very quiet.

The Village Shop

Before returning to Ismark's, the group visited the village's only shop. The proprietor was a study in cheerful dishonesty — always smiling, aggressively optimistic about the quality of goods that did not merit optimism, and pricing things with the confidence of a man who knows he is the only option for fifty miles. They bought what they needed, paid too much, and left with the faint sense of having been robbed by someone who genuinely enjoyed it.

Back at the mansion, Ireena had a bag ready. Kolyan was in the ground. Vallaki was on the map. The route existed. Strahd knew where they were.

The road would not be quiet.
